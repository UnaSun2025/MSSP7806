Primary Tasks

Choose a task, complete the checklist, and proceed to the next action.

1. Plan

Define your project concept, market fit, and operating assumptions before committing capital. Use early scenario testing to identify sensitivity to labor, energy, and scale.

  • Define product/service scope and target customers
  • Choose the most relevant industry profile
  • Set baseline assumptions for investment, labor, land, and capacity
  • Review county-level market context and concentration signals
  • Run a first-pass five-year scenario and compare alternatives
  • Document key risks and decision gates for go/no-go
Start here: Industry Profiles

2. Register

Establish legal and tax identity so your project can transact, hire, and apply for programs. Complete entity, tax, and account setup in the correct order.

  • Select legal entity structure and ownership format
  • Register through Pennsylvania Business One-Stop
  • Set up state tax accounts and payroll administration
  • Confirm local business licensing obligations
  • Set compliance calendar for recurring filings
  • Prepare baseline records for incentive applications
Start here: Method & Setup Guidance

3. Permits & Zoning

Validate land-use compatibility and permitting requirements early to avoid schedule risk. Coordinate local, DEP, and utility pathways before final design lock.

  • Shortlist counties and municipalities with suitable zoning
  • Confirm UCC and local land development requirements
  • Screen DEP triggers: air, water, stormwater, waste, and encroachment
  • Estimate approval timeline ranges and critical path milestones
  • Engage utility/interconnection contacts where needed
  • Track public engagement and community-risk factors
Start here: Geography & Site Context

4. Hire & Operate

Build a workforce and operating model that can sustain compliance, quality, and productivity. Plan staffing structure with realistic wage and training assumptions.

  • Define technical, semi-skilled, and management headcount mix
  • Set wage bands and hiring phasing by ramp period
  • Assess labor supply by county and commuting shed
  • Establish core SOP, safety, and quality controls
  • Validate operating cost profile against scenario outputs
  • Set KPI dashboard for labor, utilization, and margin
Start here: Scale & Growth Signals

5. Incentives & Funding

Align project design with available programs and financing pathways. Treat incentives as conditional until eligibility and award terms are confirmed.

  • Screen KOZ/KIZ relevance by site geography
  • Assess fit for MTC, R&D credit, and PA EDGE pathways
  • Estimate incentive impact using conservative scenarios
  • Document eligibility evidence and required submissions
  • Align application timing with permitting timeline
  • Define funding stack and contingency assumptions
Start here: Policy & Incentive Context
